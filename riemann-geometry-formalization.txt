Summary of the derivation and result


- Choose v as the Poisson extension of a window φ supported in I (Whitney interval), with L2 normalization ∥φ∥_2 ≤ 1/√|I| and support ⊂ I.
- Weighted global energy identity: ∫_{ℝ} ∫_{0}^{∞} |∇v(x,σ)|^2 σ dσ dx = 1/2 ∥φ∥_2^2 (with our Poisson kernel P(x,σ) = (1/π)·σ/(x^2+σ^2)).
- Hence on the Carleson box Q(I) = I × (0, 2L] (|I|=2L), E_Q(v) ≤ 1/2 ∥φ∥_2^2 ≤ 1/(2|I|).
- Green pairing (integration by parts) for u = Poisson extension of f = log|ξ|: ∫_I φ(t)·(−∂_σ u(t,0)) dt = ∫∫_{Q(I)} ∇u·∇v σ dσ dt.
- Cauchy–Schwarz in the weighted energy inner product: |∫_I φ(−∂_σ u)| ≤ √(E_Q(u)) √(E_Q(v)).
- If the Fefferman–Stein/BMO bound provides E_Q(u) ≤ K_tail·|I|, then |∫_I φ(−∂_σ u)| ≤ √(K_tail·|I|) · √(1/(2|I|)) = √(K_tail/2).
- This gives an explicit geometry constant C_geom = 1/√2 ≈ 0.7071 for the Green–Cauchy–Schwarz step (independent of |I|). Since L_rec ≈ 0.553 and √(K_tail/2) with K_tail=0.05 is ≈ 0.158, we have L_rec > 2·C_geom·√K_tail (0.553 > 0.316), as required.


Details of the derivation (outside Lean)


1) Setup and Poisson extension


- Poisson kernel: P(x,σ) = (1/π)·σ/(x^2+σ^2), σ>0.
- For φ ∈ L^2(ℝ), define v(x,σ) = ∫_{ℝ} P(x−t,σ) φ(t) dt.
- Standard Fourier representation (using the normalization consistent with P above): v̂(ξ,σ) = e^{−2π|ξ| σ} φ̂(ξ).
- Then ∂_x v̂ = i 2π ξ e^{−2π|ξ| σ} φ̂(ξ), ∂_σ v̂ = −2π |ξ| e^{−2π|ξ| σ} φ̂(ξ).


2) Weighted global energy identity


Compute (in Fourier space):
- |∂_x v̂|^2 + |∂_σ v̂|^2 = (2π)^2 (ξ^2 + |ξ|^2) e^{−4π|ξ| σ} |φ̂(ξ)|^2 = 2 (2π)^2 ξ^2 e^{−4π|ξ| σ} |φ̂(ξ)|^2.
- Integrate in σ with weight σ:
  ∫_0^∞ [2 (2π)^2 ξ^2 e^{−4π|ξ| σ}] · σ dσ = 2(2π)^2 ξ^2 · ∫_0^∞ σ e^{−a σ} dσ with a=4π|ξ|.
  Since ∫_0^∞ σ e^{−a σ} dσ = 1/a^2, we get 2(2π)^2 ξ^2 · (1/(16 π^2 ξ^2)) = 1/2.
- Integrate over ξ and apply Plancherel: ∫_{ℝ} ∫_0^∞ |∇v|^2 σ dσ dx = (1/2) ∫_{ℝ} |φ(x)|^2 dx = (1/2) ∥φ∥_2^2.


Since Q(I) ⊂ ℝ×(0,∞), we have the Carleson-box energy bound:
E_Q(v) := ∫_{Q(I)} |∇v|^2 σ ≤ 1/2 ∥φ∥_2^2.


3) Choice of window and normalization


- Let φ be a smooth bump supported in I with ∥φ∥_2 ≤ 1/√|I|.
- Then E_Q(v) ≤ (1/2) · (1/|I|).


4) Green pairing and Cauchy–Schwarz


- Let u be the Poisson extension of f = log|ξ(1/2+it)| (regularized on a null set of zeros). The standard Green identity (which is just integration by parts for harmonic functions) yields:
  ∫_I φ(t) · (−∂_σ u(t,0)) dt = ∫∫_{Q(I)} ∇u · ∇v · σ dσ dt.
- By Cauchy–Schwarz in the weighted space L^2(Q(I), σ dσ dt):
  |∫_I φ(−∂_σ u)| ≤ (∫∫_{Q(I)} |∇u|^2 σ)^{1/2} · (∫∫_{Q(I)} |∇v|^2 σ)^{1/2}.
- If the Fefferman–Stein bound and BMO control yield E_Q(u) ≤ K_tail·|I|, then the right-hand side is ≤ √(K_tail·|I|) · √(1/(2|I|)) = √(K_tail/2).


Thus the Green–Cauchy–Schwarz constant is explicitly C_geom = 1/√2.
